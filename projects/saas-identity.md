# SaaS & Identity Security Program (GSuite, Okta, Slack)

## Objective
Establish a secure and scalable identity and access management framework
for SaaS platforms while enabling seamless collaboration across remote
and distributed teams.

## Business Context
The organization relied heavily on SaaS platforms for communication,
identity, and business operations. A centralized governance model was
required to reduce access risks, support rapid growth, and ensure
compliance without impacting productivity.

## Platforms Covered
- Google Workspace (GSuite)
- Okta (Identity Provider)
- Slack (Collaboration)
- Other business-critical SaaS applications

## Key Responsibilities
- Defined SaaS security standards and access control policies
- Designed identity lifecycle management for joiners, movers, and leavers
- Implemented centralized authentication and authorization controls
- Partnered with HR and business teams to align access with roles

## Security Controls Implemented
- Enforced Single Sign-On (SSO) across SaaS platforms
- Mandatory Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for privileged and remote access
- Role-based access control (RBAC) and least-privilege principles
- Periodic access reviews and recertification
- Secure offboarding workflows to prevent orphaned accounts

## Governance & Compliance
- Established SaaS access review cadence (quarterly)
- Documented access approval and exception handling process
- Aligned controls with GDPR and internal security policies

## Outcomes
- Reduced unauthorized access and identity-related risks
- Improved onboarding and offboarding efficiency
- Increased visibility and control over SaaS usage
- Enabled secure collaboration for remote teams
